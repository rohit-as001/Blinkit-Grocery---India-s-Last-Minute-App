# Blinkit-Grocery---India-s-Last-Minute-App
### This Repository has Blinkit Grocery - India's Last Minute Apps projects.



# Project Overview

This project analyzes the performance of a quick-commerce grocery platform (Blinkit) using an interactive dashboard.
The dashboard provides insights into sales trends, product categories, outlet performance, customer preferences, and ratings, enabling stakeholders to make informed, data-driven decisions.

Users can dynamically filter the data by Item Type, Outlet Size, and Fat Content, making the dashboard highly interactive and insightful.



## Business Objectives
- Track overall sales, average sales, and number of items
- Analyze product category performance
- Evaluate outlet size and location impact on sales
- Understand customer preferences (fat content, item type)
- Monitor ratings and customer satisfaction
- Identify high-performing outlet types and locations



## Key KPIs
- Total Sales: ₹1.20M
- Average Sales: ₹141
- Number of Items: 1,559
- Average Rating: 3.97
- Total Transactions: 8,523
- Average Customer Rating: 3.97 / 5
- Fat Content Categories: Low Fat, Regular
- Item Types Tracked: 16 (Fruits, Snacks, Dairy, Meat, etc.)
- Establishment Years Covered: 2016 – 2024



# Key Insights

1) Product Category Performance

- Fruits & Vegetables (₹178.1K) and Snack Foods (₹175.4K) are the two highest-grossing categories, together accounting for nearly 30% of   all sales. Household items (₹136K) and Frozen Foods (₹118.6K) follow. At the bottom, Seafood (₹9.1K) and Breakfast items (₹15.6K) are    significantly underperforming.
- Categories like Seafood and Breakfast items contribute very little to total sales.


2) Fat Content Preference

  - Regular products (65%) dominate sales compared to Low Fat (35%).
  - Indicates customer preference for standard grocery products over health-focused alternatives.


3) Outlet Size Analysis

  - Medium-sized outlets (42%) generate the highest sales.
  - Followed by Small (37%) and High (21%).
  - Suggests optimal performance in medium-sized store formats.


4) Outlet Location Insights

  - Tier 3 cities (~₹472K) contribute the highest sales.
  - Followed by Tier 2 and Tier 1, indicating strong demand in smaller cities.

    
5️) Outlet Type Performance

  - Supermarket Type 1 leads in total sales and number of items.
  - Grocery stores show good item variety but slightly lower overall sales.


6️) Sales Trend by Establishment Year

  - Sales show a fluctuating pattern, with a peak in 2024 (~₹263K).
  - Indicates recent growth and expansion success.


7) Fat Content — Regular Wins

  - Regular fat products dominate with ₹776.32K in sales (65%), more than double the ₹425.36K (35%) from Low Fat items. Despite growing      health awareness, customers are still primarily purchasing regular fat products — which could reflect product mix, pricing, or       
    availability rather than preference alone.


8) Outlet Growth Trend (2016–2024)

  - Sales by outlet establishment year grew from 2016 (₹132K) to a peak in 2018 (₹204.5K), dipped in 2020 (possibly COVID-19 impact),        and recovered to a new high in 2024 (₹262.75K) — signaling long-term business resilience.


9) Consistently High Ratings

  - All outlet types maintain average ratings between 3.95 and 3.99, reflecting strong and consistent customer satisfaction across the       board.



## Business Recommendations

1. Focus on High-Performing Cities
  - Increase stock and promotions for Fruits, Vegetables, and Snack Foods.
  - Reevaluate low-performing categories like Seafood and Breakfast items.

2. Optimize Product Portfolio
  - Expand medium-sized outlets, as they generate the highest revenue.
  - Invest more in Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities, where demand is strong.

3. Product Strategy Improvement
  - Introduce more value-added or bundled products in low-performing categories.
  - Explore healthy alternatives to increase Low Fat product sales.

4. Improve Customer Experience
  - Maintain and improve average rating (3.97) by focusing on delivery speed and product quality.
  - Monitor customer feedback for continuous improvement.
    
5. Pricing & Sales Optimization
  - Use dynamic pricing strategies based on demand patterns.
  - Offer targeted discounts to boost underperforming segments.

6. Scale Medium-Sized Outlets
  - Medium outlets deliver 42% of total sales. Management should use medium-format stores as the standard template for new outlet            launches rather than over-investing in large-format High-size outlets.

7. Invest More in Tier 3 Expansion
  - Tier 3 cities are the top revenue market. Management should expand outlet coverage, increase SKU range, and run hyperlocal campaigns     in Tier 3 cities to further capitalize on this growth engine.

8. Fix Underperforming Categories
  - Seafood (₹9.1K), Breakfast (₹15.6K), and Starchy Foods (₹21.9K) are consistently weak. Management should determine whether this is a     supply, pricing, or demand issue and act accordingly — either invest to grow or reallocate shelf space to top performers.

9. Boost Item Visibility Scores
  - Average item visibility is very low (0.06–0.10). Management should invest in in-app product placement, featured category slots, and      promotional placements to push high-margin, low-visibility items to the forefront.

10. Sustain the 2024 Growth Momentum
  - The 2024 peak of ₹262.75K is the highest on record. Management should identify the drivers behind this surge — new outlets, product      expansion, or promotions — and replicate those strategies to sustain the growth trajectory.
